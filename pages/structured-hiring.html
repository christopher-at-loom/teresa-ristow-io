The Problem with Unstructured Interviews

Most hiring interviews are conversations, not assessments. Research consistently shows that unstructured interviews have low predictive validity for job performance. Interviewers form impressions in the first few minutes and spend the rest of the conversation confirming those impressions.

The result: hiring decisions driven by rapport and gut feeling rather than evidence. This leads to higher turnover, lower performance, and increased legal risk from inconsistent evaluation criteria.

What You Will Learn

  • How to conduct a job analysis that identifies the competencies that actually predict success
  • How to write behavioral and situational interview questions tied to job-relevant criteria
  • How to build and calibrate rating scales that reduce interviewer bias
  • How to train interview panels for consistency and inter-rater reliability
  • How to evaluate and improve your hiring process with validity evidence
  • Legal and ethical considerations for defensible selection procedures

Who This Course Is For

  • HR professionals responsible for designing or improving hiring processes
  • Hiring managers who want to make better selection decisions
  • Talent acquisition teams looking to reduce bias and improve quality of hire
  • I/O psychology students and practitioners seeking a practical framework grounded in research

About the Instructor

Teresa Ristow holds a PhD in Industrial/Organizational Psychology from Virginia Tech. She has taught graduate-level courses in employee selection and has published research on assessment methodology. Her work bridges the gap between what the science says about hiring and what organizations actually do.
