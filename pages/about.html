Teresa Ristow is an Industrial/Organizational (I/O) psychologist, researcher, and educator dedicated to making workplace psychology research accessible and actionable for practitioners.

Education and Research

Teresa holds a PhD in Industrial/Organizational Psychology from Virginia Tech, one of the nation's leading programs in the field. Her research focuses on the intersection of artificial intelligence and human resources, including the development of the AIRA (AI Readiness Assessment) framework for evaluating organizational preparedness for AI-driven HR tools.

Her methodological expertise spans natural language processing (NLP) for job advertisement analysis, survey design and validation, and advanced quantitative methods. Her work has been published in Psychological Methods, Behavior Research Methods, and other peer-reviewed outlets.

Teaching

Teresa has taught graduate-level courses in I/O psychology, covering core topics including:

  • Employee selection and structured hiring
  • Performance management systems
  • Training and development
  • Organizational behavior
  • Research methods and psychometrics

Her teaching philosophy centers on bridging the gap between academic research and real-world application, helping students and professionals alike develop evidence-based approaches to workplace challenges.

Mission

Too often, important findings in I/O psychology remain locked in academic journals, inaccessible to the practitioners who could benefit most. Teresa's courses and writing aim to change that by translating rigorous research into clear, practical frameworks that HR professionals, people managers, and job seekers can apply immediately.

Whether you are building a structured interview process, designing a performance management system, or preparing for your next career move, the goal is the same: better outcomes through better science.
