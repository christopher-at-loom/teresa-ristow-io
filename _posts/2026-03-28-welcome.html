Welcome. I am Teresa Ristow, an Industrial/Organizational psychologist, researcher, and educator. This blog is a new home for ideas, frameworks, and findings from the science of work, written for the people who can put them to use.

Industrial/Organizational psychology has a communication problem. The field produces rigorous, valuable research on hiring, performance management, training, leadership, and motivation. But most of that research stays locked behind journal paywalls and written in a style that assumes a PhD-level audience. Practitioners, the HR professionals, hiring managers, and career coaches who could benefit most, rarely see it.

This blog exists to bridge that gap. Here you will find research-backed perspectives on topics like structured interviewing, job analysis, assessment design, survey methodology, and the growing role of AI in human resources. Every post will connect peer-reviewed evidence to practical application: what the science says, why it matters, and how to act on it.

I will also share updates on my courses, which cover two verticals: tools for employers and HR professionals building better talent systems, and resources for job seekers and career coaches navigating the hiring process from the other side. Whether you are designing a selection system or preparing for a behavioral interview, the underlying science is the same, and understanding it gives you an advantage.

If you work in HR, manage people, study I/O psychology, or are navigating a career transition, I hope you will find something useful here. Subscribe or check back regularly as new content goes up.
